The Problem

Pollution is a major problem in today's world, and it is often mentioned in the media. Here are some terrifying facts:

  • Over two tons of garbage have to be removed from the streets in Oslo every day.
  • Around 80% of all the plastic that ends up in rivers and the ocean comes from mainland.
  • It is estimated that by 2050, the ocean will contain more plastic than fish.
  • A plastic bottle can take up to 450 years or more to decompose.
  • Plastic bags take between 10 to 1000 years to decompose.

Why does this happen? Is it because people are lazy or is it because there is a lack of knowledge in our society?

In Norway there are different garbage cans to help sort different types of garbage, which makes it easier to recycle the waste. However, many adults are still unsure about where to throw the different types of garbage. There are for example many people who throw food wrapped in plastic in the compost, or dirty plastic in the plastic trash.

If you are not an animal lover, maybe you love yourself or your family. When you picture plastic you probably only picture the larger things you can see with your eyes like plastic bottles or plastic bags. Have you heard about microplastic? It is small plastic particles which are impossible to see with the human eye. These particles get in our food and drinking water. To sum up, if you do not recycle you can harm yourself and your loved ones.

Another problem is that many people drive to work or school instead of using public transport, riding a bicycle or walking. CO2 pollution is bad for our environment. And when driving alone in a car, it leads to more CO2 pollution, which could have been prevented. Even though Donald Trump may think this is fake news, it most certainly is not!

The solution

To reduce the pollution there are many things you can do:

  • Throw away your garbage, it does not belong in the streets
  • Throw garbage in the right trash
  • Reduce use of plastic
  • Reuse plastic bags
  • Recycle bottles
  • Ride a bicycle or walk instead of driving to work/school
  • Use public transportation
  • RECYCLE
