The problem

Pollution is a major problem today and is mentioned in the media. Here are some facts:

  • Over two tons of garbage have to be removed from the streets in Oslo every day.
  • Around 80% of all the plastics that gets in rivers and the ocean comes from land.
  • It is estimated that by 2050, the ocean will contain more plastic than fish.
  • A plastic bottle can take up to 450 years or more to decompose.
  • Plastic bags take between 10 to 1000 years to decompose.

Why do this happen? Is it because people are lazy or is it because of lack of knowledge?

In Norway there are different garbage cans to help sort different types of garbage. But many adults are still unsure about where to throw different garbage. Many people throw food with plastic in compost, or dirty plastic in plastic cans.

If you're not an animal lover, maybe you love yourself or your family. When you picture plastic you probably only picture the larger things you can see with your eyes like plastic bottles or plastic bags. Have you heard about microplastic? This is small plastic particles that's hard to see and this can get in our drinking water and food if we don't recycle.

Another problem is that many people drive to work or school instead of using public transportation, riding a bicycle or walking. CO2 pollution is bad for our environment. And when driving alone in a car, it leads to more CO2 pollution.

The solution

To reduce the pollution there are many things you can do:

  • Stop throwing garbage on the streets.
  • Throw garbage in the right can
  • Reduce use of plastic
  • Reuse plastic bags
  • Recycle bottles
  • Ride a bicycle or walk instead of driving
  • Use public transportation
  • RECYCLE
